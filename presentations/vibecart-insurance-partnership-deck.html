A short note for your team

Hi — from the VibeCart team.

We built VibeCart as a living marketplace: people discover products, book services, and pay in a way that feels modern and calm. We’re not here to replace your actuaries or your licences — we’re asking whether we can send you the right people, at the right moment, in a way your compliance team can live with.

This deck is about partnership shapes that already exist in the real world: affiliate links, referrals, or a careful middle layer — whatever fits your rules where you operate.

Why we’re talking to insurers at all

You’re not looking for “more internet.” You’re looking for signal.

Most acquisition is loud. Someone scrolls past an ad and forgets it. What’s different is when someone has already decided to do something — book a trip, buy something that matters, lock in a service — and cover is a sensible next question, not a cold pitch.

VibeCart sits in that quieter space: intent, baskets, bookings, messages between real humans. If your products belong in that conversation, we’d rather hand people to your journey than pretend we’re selling policies off the side of a webpage.

What you’d actually be looking at

VibeCart today — in plain words

There’s a storefront side: categories, sellers, checkout, the boring-but-important stuff like Stripe so money moves cleanly. There’s also a “My Business” lane where stylists and similar providers list offers, publish times, and handle booking requests with clients.

Behind that is the usual grown-up layer: policies, user acknowledgements where it matters, hosting that doesn’t embarrass you in an IT review. Nothing magic — just intentional product work with room to wire in offers and reminders the day you say yes.

Ways we could actually work together

Pick the shape that matches your risk appetite

Affiliate or marketing — You treat us like a channel. We send traffic or tracked outcomes; your team owns wording, pricing, and the sale.

Referral / “middleman” in the honest sense — We show education or a soft prompt and send interested users into your quote or sales flow. Labels stay honest: you’re the insurer; we’re the front door.

Deeper integration later — If the law and your comfort allow, we can talk APIs and embedded journeys. That’s a second chapter, not a first conversation.

What’s in it for you

We’re biased, but here’s the honest pitch

You get reach without shouting: people who are already doing something on the site, in a context where insurance can feel relevant instead of random.

You keep control — product rules, disclosures, who is allowed to say what. We’re asking for a partnership, not a takeover.

And if we do this properly, we both get something we can measure, with data handled the way your privacy people expect. No hand-wavy “trust us.”

Trust — we know that word gets overused

So here’s what we mean by it

We care that the site doesn’t feel like a sketchy corner of the web. There are policy pages, payment paths that don’t wander off into random links, and a production setup we’re willing to put in front of someone who wears a risk hat.

Anything that touches insurance for real should go through your legal and compliance sign-off. We’re building the rails; you’re the regulated part. That division matters to us too.

Money and pace

Let’s not pretend we have a term sheet yet

Commercials can be simple: affiliate-style fees, a fixed sponsorship, or a mix. What we care about is that customers aren’t squeezed for gimmicks, and that your economics still make sense.

We’re happy to start small — one product, one geography, one funnel — and look at numbers after a few months like adults.
